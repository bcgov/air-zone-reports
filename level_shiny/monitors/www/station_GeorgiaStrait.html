Monitoring in the Georgia Strait Air Zone

There are 17 air quality monitoring stations in the Georgia Strait Air Zone that were active from 2019 to 2021. These stations were operated by Industry (9 stations), and the Ministry of Environment and Climate Change Strategy (ENV) (8 stations).

The Georgia Strait has 7 locations where the Air Quality Health Index (AQHI) are reported. AQHI is a communication tool used to report the health impacts of poor air quality. It is calculated from the measured levels of fine particulate matter(PM2.5), ground-level ozone(O3), and nitrogen dioxide(NO2).
