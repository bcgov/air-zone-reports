Monitoring in the Central Interior Air Zone

There are 11 air quality monitoring stations in the Central Interior Air Zone that were active from 2019 to 2021. These stations were operated by Industry (3 stations), and the Ministry of Environment and Climate Change Strategy (ENV) (8 stations).

The Central Interior has 4 locations where the Air Quality Health Index (AQHI) are reported. AQHI is a communication tool used to report the health impacts of poor air quality. It is calculated from the measured levels of fine particulate matter(PM2.5), ground-level ozone(O3), and nitrogen dioxide(NO2).
